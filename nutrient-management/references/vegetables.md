## 1. SNS for Vegetables

Uses the same Field Assessment Method as arable crops with the same soil categories, rainfall tables, and SNS lookup tables. See principles.md for the main SNS tables.

### Vegetable N Residue Categories
When vegetables are the previous crop, classify residues:
- **High N vegetables:** Calabrese, brussels sprouts, cauliflower (where crop debris is returned to soil), broccoli
- **Medium N vegetables:** Lettuce, leeks, long-season brassicas, Dutch white cabbage
- **Low N vegetables:** Carrots, onions, radishes, swedes, turnips, parsnips

On medium, deep silty or deep clayey soils, nitrogen residues from vegetables can persist for several years, especially in drier areas. The SNS Index may need upward adjustment.

## 3. Nitrogen — Other Vegetables

### Asparagus

| Year | N recommendation (kg/ha) |
|------|-------------------------|
| Establishment (year 1) | 0 |
| Year 2 | 50–75 |
| Year 3+ (established) | 100–150 |

Apply N after cutting finishes (late June) and again in early August if growth is strong. At SNS 3+, reduce by 25–50 kg/ha.

### Celery

| Crop | SNS 0 | SNS 1 | SNS 2 | SNS 3 | SNS 4+ |
|------|-------|-------|-------|-------|--------|
| Celery | 325 | 275 | 225 | 175 | 0–100 |

Apply in multiple splits. Max 100 kg N/ha at planting, remainder as top dressings.

### Peas and Beans (fresh market/vining/freezing)

| Crop | N recommendation (kg/ha) |
|------|-------------------------|
| Vining peas | 0 (or up to 20 on very low SNS/light soils) |
| Broad beans | 0–20 |
| French/runner beans | 0–50 depending on SNS |

Peas and beans fix their own N. Small starter N (up to 20 kg/ha) may help establishment.

## 4. Phosphate, Potash and Magnesium

P and K recommendations for vegetables vary by crop. General principles:

| Soil Index | P2O5 range (kg/ha) | K2O range (kg/ha) |
|------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| 0 | 150–300 | 200–350 |
| 1 | 100–200 | 150–250 |
| 2 | 50–100 (maintenance) | 75–150 (maintenance) |
| 3+ | 0 | 0 |

Higher rates within the range for: brassicas, celery, leeks, potatoes. Lower rates for: carrots, onions, peas, radish.

**Magnesium:** At Mg Index 0–1, apply 50–150 kg MgO/ha. Important for brassicas and lettuce.

## 5. Sulphur and Micronutrients

### Sulphur
Brassicas are particularly responsive to sulphur. Apply 50–75 kg SO3/ha to brassica crops on soils at risk of deficiency (sandy, shallow, high winter rainfall).

### Micronutrients
| Nutrient | Crops at risk | Conditions | Treatment |
|----------|-------------|------------|-----------|
| Boron (B) | Brassicas, beetroot, celery | Sandy soils, high pH, low OM | 1–2 kg B/ha in seedbed |
| Manganese (Mn) | Peas, beans, brassicas, beetroot | High pH, sandy, organic soils | Foliar sprays |
| Molybdenum (Mo) | Brassicas (whiptail) | Acid soils pH <5.5 | Lime to pH 6.5, or foliar Mo |
| Iron (Fe) | Not common in vegetables | Very calcareous soils | Foliar applications |
