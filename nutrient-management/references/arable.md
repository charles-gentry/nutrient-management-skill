*May exceed NVZ N max limit. N max is for the whole farm area of a crop type, not individual fields.

**Yield adjustment:** For yields above 8 t/ha, increase by 10 kg N/ha per 0.5 t/ha additional yield (max 13 t/ha). For lower yields, reduce by 10 kg N/ha per 0.5 t/ha.

**Bread wheat:** Extra 40 kg N/ha can increase grain protein by up to 1%. Apply during stem extension (GS32–GS39).

**Timing:** No seedbed N needed. <120 kg N/ha: single dressing by early stem extension. ≥120 kg N/ha: split — ~40 kg N/ha mid-Feb to early March, remainder in late March to early April.

## 3. Nitrogen Recommendations — Other Arable

### Sugar Beet
N recommendations for sugar beet depend on soil type and SNS Index. All mineral soils typically 80–120 kg N/ha at SNS 0–1, reducing to 0 at high SNS. Consult Section 4 of RB209 for full table.

### Peas and Beans
Peas and beans fix their own nitrogen and generally do not require N fertiliser. Small amounts (up to 20 kg N/ha) may help establishment on light soils with low SNS.

### Biomass Crops
Miscanthus and SRC willow/poplar have minimal fertiliser needs once established. Modest N (40–60 kg N/ha) may be applied to SRC after the first year.

## 6. Sulphur Recommendations

### Cereals
Sulphur risk depends on soil texture and winter rainfall:

| Soil texture | Low rainfall (<175mm Nov–Feb) | Medium (175–375mm) | High (>375mm) |
|-------------|-------------------------------|--------------------|--------------|
| Sandy | High risk | High risk | High risk |
| Loamy/coarse silty | Low risk | High risk | High risk |
| Clay/fine silty/peaty | Low risk | Low risk | High risk |

Where risk is HIGH: apply 25–50 kg SO3/ha between early March and end of April.
For bread wheat: applying 50 kg SO3/ha can reduce acrylamide formation.

### Oilseeds
Apply 50–80 kg SO3/ha to all winter and spring OSR on mineral soils (late Feb to early March).

## 7. Micronutrient Deficiency — Arable

### Cereals
| Nutrient | Risk factors | Soil analysis threshold | Leaf analysis threshold |
|----------|-------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| Manganese (Mn) | Sandy soils pH >7.5; organic/peaty pH >6.0; over-liming; low rainfall | Not reliable | <20 mg Mn/kg |
| Copper (Cu) | Shallow over chalk; high OM; sandy/peat soils | EDTA <1.0 mg Cu/l (or <2.5 if OM >6%) | Not reliable |
| Zinc (Zn) | Sandy soils; high pH; P Index 5+ | EDTA <1.5 mg Zn/l | <15 mg Zn/kg |

### Oilseed Rape
| Nutrient | Risk factors | Soil analysis threshold | Leaf threshold |
|----------|-------------|------------------------|---------------|
| Manganese (Mn) | Same as cereals | Not reliable | <20 mg Mn/kg |
| Boron (B) | Sandy; high OM; pH >7; over-liming | Hot water <0.8 mg B/l | <20 mg B/kg |
| Molybdenum (Mo) | Soil pH <6.5 | Ammonium oxalate <0.1 mg Mo/l | Insufficient data |

**Treatment:** Apply micronutrient fertilisers as soil or foliar applications. Foliar nutrients can be tank-mixed with fungicides for cost-effectiveness but check label compatibility.
