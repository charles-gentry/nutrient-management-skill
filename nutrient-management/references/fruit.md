## 1. Before Planting

Apply P and K before planting based on soil analysis. Lime to target pH (6.5 for most fruit; 5.5–6.0 for blueberries).

### Pre-planting Recommendations (kg/ha)

| Nutrient | Index 0 | Index 1 | Index 2 | Index 3+ |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|----------|
| P2O5 | 150 | 100 | 50 | 0 |
| K2O | 200 | 150 | 75 | 0 |
| MgO (if Mg Index 0–1) | 200 | 100 | 0 | 0 |

Incorporate into the top 20–30cm before planting. Also apply bulky organic matter (FYM, compost) to improve soil structure.

## 2. Top Fruit (Established Orchards)

### Nitrogen for Established Orchards

| Soil type | N recommendation (kg/ha) |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Light sand soils | 50–80 |
| Other mineral soils | 30–60 |
| Grass alley orchards — light soils | 75–100 |
| Grass alley orchards — other soils | 50–80 |

Apply N in early spring (February–March). Reduce or omit if tree vigour is excessive. Increase if growth is weak.

Orchards with complete grass cover: apply higher rates as grass competes for N.

### Phosphate and Potash for Top Fruit

| Nutrient | Index 0 | Index 1 | Index 2 | Index 3+ |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|----------|
| P2O5 | 60 | 40 | 20 | 0 |
| K2O | 100 | 75 | 50 | 0 |

Apply K as muriate of potash (KCl) or sulphate of potash in autumn/winter. Apply P in autumn.

**Magnesium:** If Mg Index 0–1 or leaf analysis shows deficiency, apply 50–100 kg MgO/ha or foliar Epsom salt sprays during the season.

## 3. Soft Fruit and Vines

### Nitrogen

| Crop | N recommendation (kg/ha) |
|------|-------------------------|
| Blackcurrants | 80–120 |
| Raspberries | 60–100 |
| Gooseberries | 50–80 |
| Blueberries | 30–60 (use ammonium-based N; avoid nitrate) |
| Grapes (outdoor vines) | 30–60 |
| Blackberries | 50–80 |

Apply N in early spring. Split dressings may benefit: 50% in March, 50% in May.

### Phosphate and Potash for Soft Fruit

| Nutrient | Index 0 | Index 1 | Index 2 | Index 3+ |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|----------|
| P2O5 | 50 | 35 | 20 | 0 |
| K2O — blackcurrants/gooseberries | 150 | 100 | 50 | 0 |
| K2O — raspberries/other | 100 | 75 | 40 | 0 |

Soft fruit generally has high K demand, especially blackcurrants.

## 4. Strawberries

### Open-ground Strawberries — Nitrogen by SNS

| SNS Index | N recommendation (kg/ha) |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| 0 | 100–125 |
| 1 | 75–100 |
| 2 | 50–75 |
| 3 | 25–50 |
| 4+ | 0–25 |

Apply 50% pre-planting/early spring, 50% as top dress during early growth. Do not over-apply N — excessive growth promotes Botrytis.

### Open-ground Strawberries — P, K, Mg

| Nutrient | Index 0 | Index 1 | Index 2 | Index 3+ |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|----------|
| P2O5 | 100 | 60 | 30 | 0 |
| K2O | 175 | 125 | 75 | 0 |
| MgO (Index 0–1) | 75 | 50 | 0 | 0 |

### Substrate Strawberry Production
Nutrient solutions for substrate (tabletop) strawberry production follow specific fertigation recipes. Typical target concentrations in the feed solution (mg/L):

| Nutrient | Vegetative | Flowering | Fruiting |
|----------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| N (as NO3) | 150–180 | 120–150 | 100–130 |
| P | 30–40 | 30–40 | 30–40 |
| K | 200–250 | 250–300 | 300–350 |
| Ca | 120–150 | 120–150 | 120–150 |
| Mg | 30–40 | 30–40 | 30–40 |

Maintain EC at 1.2–1.8 mS/cm and pH 5.5–6.5. Adjust based on run-off analysis.

## 5. Hops

### Nitrogen

| Soil type | N recommendation (kg/ha) |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Light sand soils | 175–200 |
| Other mineral soils | 150–175 |

Apply in 2–3 splits: first at stringing (April), second at training (May), optional third in June.

### Phosphate, Potash, Magnesium

| Nutrient | Index 0 | Index 1 | Index 2 | Index 3+ |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|----------|
| P2O5 | 110 | 80 | 50 | 0 |
| K2O | 250 | 200 | 150 | 0 |
| MgO (Index 0–1) | 100 | 50 | 0 | 0 |

Hops have a very high K demand. Sulphate of potash is preferred over muriate.

Apply 50 kg SO3/ha to hops on soils at risk of sulphur deficiency.

### Apple Fruit Analysis
Apple fruit mineral analysis (at harvest) can indicate storage quality:

| Nutrient | Good storability | Risk of disorders |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| N | <60 mg N/100ml juice | >80 mg N/100ml: increased rot risk |
| K | <120 mg K/100ml juice | >150 mg K/100ml: increased bitter pit risk |
| Ca | >4.5 mg Ca/100ml juice | <4.0 mg Ca/100ml: high bitter pit risk |
| Mg | 4–8 mg Mg/100ml juice | <3 mg Mg/100ml: poor quality |
| P | 8–15 mg P/100ml juice | <6 mg P/100ml: low vigour |

K:Ca ratio should be <30 for good storage. Calcium sprays (e.g. calcium chloride) during the growing season can improve storability.
