## 2. Soil Categories for SNS Assessment

| Soil category | Description | Nitrogen properties |
|---------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Light sand soils | Sand, loamy sand or sandy loam to 40cm; sand/loamy sand 40–80cm, or over sandstone | Poor water-holding, retain little N |
| Shallow soils | Over impermeable rock (chalk, limestone) within 40cm. Sandy over sandstone = light sand | Less able to retain or supply N at depth |
| Medium soils | Medium-textured mineral soils not fitting other categories | Moderate N retention, average rooting depth |
| Deep clayey soils | Clay/clay loam topsoil over clay subsoil >40cm | Retain more N than lighter soils |
| Deep silty soils | Silt loam/silty clay loam to 100cm+. Marine alluvium, warp, brickearth | Retain more N, deeper rooting |
| Organic soils | Predominantly mineral, 10–20% organic matter. Dark, stains fingers | More N retention, higher mineralisation |
| Peat soils | More than 20% organic matter from sedge or similar | Very high N mineralisation potential |

### Soil Category Flowchart
1. Mainly peat? → Peat soil
2. Dark, stains fingers black/grey? → Organic soil
3. Over chalk/limestone/rock within 40cm? → Shallow soil
4. Rough and gritty? → If subsoil sand/sandstone: Light sand. Otherwise continue.
5. Smooth and buttery? → If silty to 100cm+: Deep silty. Otherwise: Medium soil.
6. Rolls into strong ball? → If subsoil clay: Deep clayey. Otherwise: Medium soil.
7. Default → Medium soil

For Organic and Peat soils at SNS Index 3+ — consult a FACTS Qualified Adviser.

**Vegetable N categories:**
- High N: leafy brassicas (calabrese, brussels sprouts, cauliflower with debris returned)
- Medium N: lettuce, leeks, long-season brassicas, Dutch white cabbage
- Low N: carrots, onions, radishes, swedes, turnips

### SNS Adjustments
- **Manure history:** Regular organic manures → increase Index by 1–2 levels
- **Cover crops:** Well-established autumn-sown cover crop may increase Index
- **Fertiliser residues:** If previous crop over/under-fertilised, adjust accordingly
- **Field vegetables as previous crop:** On medium, deep silty or deep clayey soils, N residues can persist. Adjust upwards where winter rainfall is low and vegetable cropping history >1 year.

## 4. SNS Measurement Method

Use when SNS likely large and uncertain. Not recommended for peat/organic soils or first year after ploughing long leys.

SNS = SMN + Crop N + Mineralisable N adjustment

### SMN Conversion
- mg N/kg × 2 = kg N/ha per 15cm layer
- mg N/kg × 4 = kg N/ha per 30cm layer
- mg N/kg × 8 = kg N/ha per 60cm layer

### Cereal Crop N
| Shoots/m² | Autumn (kg N/ha) | Spring (kg N/ha) |
|-----------|------------------|------------------|
| 500 | 5 | 15 |
| 1,000 | 15 | 30 |
| 1,500 | 25 | 50 |

### Oilseed Rape Crop N
| GAI | Crop N (kg/ha) |
|-----|---------------|
| 0.5 | 25 |
| 1.0 | 50 |
| 2.0 | 80–100 |

### SNS Index from Measurement

| SNS (kg N/ha) | SNS Index |
|---------------|-----------|
| Less than 60 | 0 |
| 61–80 | 1 |
| 81–100 | 2 |
| 101–120 | 3 |
| 121–160 | 4 |
| 161–240 | 5 |
| More than 240 | 6 |

Minimum practical SNS = 50 kg N/ha (Index 0).

## 5. Soil pH and Liming

### Optimum Soil pH
- Arable mineral soils: 6.5 (peat 5.8)
- Grassland mineral soils: 6.0 (organic 5.7, peat 5.3)
- Aim to raise pH 0.2 above optimum
