## 1. Phosphate, Potash and Magnesium

### P, K and Mg Recommendations (kg/ha)

| Nutrient | Soil Index 0 | Index 1 | Index 2 | Index 3 | Index 4+ |
|----------|-------------|---------|---------|---------|----------|
| P2O5 | 250 | 200 | 150 | 100 | 0 |
| K2O — sandy/shallow soils | 300 | 250 | 200 | 100 | 0 |
| K2O — other soils | 250 | 200 | 150 | 100 | 0 |
| MgO — if Mg Index 0 | 150 | – | – | – | – |
| MgO — if Mg Index 1 | 100 | – | – | – | – |

Apply P as close to planting as possible (seedbed or at planting). Place P near the seed tuber for best effect.

K should be applied before or at planting. Do not band high rates of K fertiliser close to the seed tuber (chloride damage risk). On light soils, split application may reduce leaching.

MgO at Index 0–1: apply in seedbed or as kieserite. Magnesium is important for tuber quality and skin finish.

## 2. SNS Calculation for Potatoes

Uses the same Field Assessment Method as arable crops (see principles.md) with the same soil categories and rainfall tables. The SNS tables in Section 4 include potatoes as a previous crop.

### Rainfall Categories
Same as arable: Low (<150mm EWR / <600mm annual), Moderate (150–250mm / 600–700mm), High (>250mm / >700mm).

### Key differences for potato SNS:
- On organic and peat soils, SNS is typically high (Index 3–6) — consult adviser
- After grass leys, use Table 4.6 (in principles.md) for SNS following grass
- Regular manure applications increase SNS by 1–2 Index levels

## 3. Nitrogen Recommendations

1. SNS Index
2. Variety determinacy group (1–4)
3. Expected growing season length (early lifting vs. full season)

### Fine-tuning N for potatoes
- **Soil type:** On light sand soils, reduce N by 20 kg/ha (higher leaching risk, more responsive to lower N)
- **Organic matter:** High OM soils release more N — reduce recommendation
- **Processing crops:** Varieties for processing (chips/crisps) may need higher N to maintain canopy; adjust within the group range
- **Seed crops:** Reduce N by 25–40% to control tuber size
- **Early potatoes lifted <60 days:** Reduce to 50–75% of the early season recommendation

### Timing of application
Apply all N before or at planting. On sandy/shallow soils where total N >150 kg/ha, consider a split: 2/3 at planting, 1/3 as top dressing by full canopy cover.

| Vivaldi | Sante | | |

Determinate varieties: canopy senesces naturally and stops growing. Need less N.
Indeterminate varieties: continue to grow and need haulm destruction. Need more N to maintain canopy and tuber bulking.

If variety is not listed, use Group 2–3 as default and adjust based on canopy behaviour.

## 5. Sulphur and Micronutrients

### Sulphur
On soils at risk of S deficiency (sandy, shallow, high rainfall), apply 25–50 kg SO3/ha.

### Magnesium
Important for tuber skin quality. Apply at Mg Index 0–1 (see rates above).

### Micronutrients
- **Manganese:** Risk on sandy soils pH >7, organic/peat pH >6. Foliar sprays of manganese sulphate.
- **Boron:** Rarely deficient but can cause internal brown spot/hollow heart at very low levels. Apply boron to seedbed on sandy soils.
