## 1. Grass Growth Class (GGC) and SNS

### Determining GGC

| Soil available water | Soil types | Rainfall Apr–Sep: ≤300mm | 300–400mm | >400mm |
|---------------------|------------|--------------------------|-----------|--------|
| Low | Light sand, gravels, shallow (not chalk) | Very poor | Poor | Average |
| Medium | Medium, deep clayey, shallow over chalk | Poor | Average | Good |
| High | Deep silty, peaty, soils with groundwater | Average | Good | Very good |

For sites >300m altitude: reduce GGC by one class.

### SNS Status for Grassland

| Previous management | N use (kg/ha/yr) | SNS status |
|--------------------|-------------------|------------|
| Long-term grass reseeded or after 1yr arable | Over 250 | High |
| " | 100–250 or high clover | Moderate |
| " | Up to 100 | Low |
| First year ley after 2+ years arable — previous: potatoes, OSR, peas/beans, not light sand | – | Moderate |
| First year ley after 2+ years arable — previous: cereals, sugar beet, linseed, or light sand | – | Low |

**Adjustments:** N recommendations assume moderate SNS. For HIGH SNS: reduce by 30 kg N/ha. For LOW SNS: increase by 30 kg N/ha.

Increase SNS by one class if >150 kg N/ha total N regularly applied as organic manure. Reduce by one class if cut for silage and <150 kg N/ha organic manure applied on average.

## 2. Nitrogen — Grass Silage

Recommendations for grass swards >3 years with low clover and moderate SNS, on very good/good GGC sites.

| Target annual DM yield (t/ha) | 1st cut | 2nd cut | 3rd cut | 4th cut | Total N applied |
|-------------------------------|---------|---------|---------|---------|----------------|
| 5–7 | 70 | – | – | – | 70 |
| 7–9 | 80 | 50 | – | – | 130 |
| 9–12 | 100 | 75 | 75* | – | 250 |
| 12–15+ | 120 | 90 | 70* | 30* | 310 |

*Reduce or omit if growth severely restricted by drought.
Fresh yield ≈ 4× DM yield if silage is 25% DM.

**SNS adjustments for silage:**
- High SNS: −10 kg N/ha 1st cut, −20 kg N/ha 2nd cut
- Low SNS: +10 kg N/ha 1st cut, +20 kg N/ha 2nd cut

**Following early spring grazing:** reduce 1st cut recommendation by 25 kg/ha.

**Timing:** Split 1st cut rates >80 kg N/ha: 40 kg N/ha mid-Feb to early March, remainder late March to early April. Apply 2nd and subsequent cuts as soon as possible after the previous cut.

## 3. Nitrogen — Grazing

| GGC | Target DM yield (t/ha) | N recommendation (kg/ha/year) |
|-----|----------------------|------------------------------|
| Very good/Good | 8–12 | 200–280 |
| Average | 6–8 | 100–200 |
| Poor/Very poor | 4–6 | 0–100 |

Apply in 5–8 split dressings of 25–40 kg N/ha from February/March to August/September. First application: 40 kg N/ha by mid-February (south) or early March (north). Final application: no later than mid-August on most soils.

**If drought restricts growth:** reduce N by about 1 t DM/ha yield loss for every 2 weeks without growth. Reduce unused N from the season total if grass doesn't grow for 2+ weeks: ~40 kg N/ha unused.

## 4. Nitrogen — Hay

| GGC | Target DM yield | N recommendation (total) |
|-----|-----------------|-------------------------|
| Good/Very good | 5–7 t/ha | 70–100 |
| Average | 3.5–5 t/ha | 40–70 |
| Poor | 2–3.5 t/ha | 0–40 |

Apply as a single dressing in early spring. For a two-cut system, apply 50% before each cut.

## 6. Phosphate and Potash — Silage

### Table: P and K recommendations for grass silage (kg/ha)

| Cut | Nutrient | P/K Index 0 | Index 1 | Index 2 | Index 3 | Index 4+ |
|-----|----------|-------------|---------|---------|---------|----------|
| 1st cut (23 t FW/ha) | P2O5 | 100 | 70 | 40M | 20 | 0 |
| | K2O – prev autumn | 60 | 30 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| | K2O – spring | 80 | 80 | 80M(2-)/60(2+) | 30 | 0 |
| 2nd cut (15 t FW/ha) | P2O5 | 25 | 25 | 25M | 0 | 0 |
| | K2O | 120 | 100 | 90M(2-)/60(2+) | 40 | 0 |
| 3rd cut (9 t FW/ha) | P2O5 | 15 | 15 | 15M | 0 | 0 |
| | K2O | 80 | 80 | 80M(2-)/40(2+) | 20 | 0 |
| 4th cut (7 t FW/ha) | P2O5 | 10 | 10 | 10M | 0 | 0 |
| | K2O | 70 | 70 | 70M(2-)/40(2+) | 20 | 0 |

M = maintenance level. FW = fresh weight. Yields based on wilted silage at 25% DM.

To minimise luxury K uptake: max 80–90 kg K2O/ha in spring for 1st cut. Apply balance in previous autumn.

**Extra potash for cutting:** At K Index 2- or below:
- 1–2 cut systems: extra 60 kg K2O/ha after last cut or by autumn
- 3-cut systems: extra 30 kg K2O/ha after cutting
- 4-cut systems: no extra needed

## 7. Phosphate and Potash — Grazing and Hay

### Grazing

| Nutrient | Index 0 | Index 1 | Index 2 | Index 3+ |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|----------|
| P2O5 | 80 | 50 | 20 | 0 |
| K2O | 60 | 30 | 0 | 0 |

Apply P in several small applications during the season. Apply K in June/July or several small applications. At Index 0, apply 30 kg K2O/ha for first grazing. Avoid K in spring if hypomagnesaemia risk.

### Hay

| Nutrient | Index 0 | Index 1 | Index 2 | Index 3 | Over 3 |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|---------|--------|
| P2O5 | 80 | 55 | 30M | 0 | 0 |
| K2O | 140 | 115 | 90M(2-)/65(2+) | 20* | 0 |

*May be unnecessary in upper half of Index 3.

### Newly sown swards
In the first season after autumn or spring sowing, deduct amounts of P and K applied to the seedbed from the recommendations.

### Legume swards
Apply P, K and Mg as recommended for pure grass swards. Clover may benefit from small K application at Index 2-.

## 8. Sulphur Recommendations

Sulphur deficiency is common in grassland, especially in later cuts and where high N rates are used. The malate:sulphate test on herbage is useful; the critical level is 0.25% total S or N:S ratio >13:1.

**Soils at risk of S deficiency:**
- Sandy and shallow soils (chalk, limestone)
- Loamy/coarse silty soils with >200mm rainfall Nov–Feb
- Clay/fine silty/peat soils with >400mm rainfall Nov–Feb

**Application rates:**
- Silage: 40 kg SO3/ha before each cut
- Grazing: 20–30 kg SO3/ha when up to 100 kg N/ha is applied, then additional 20–30 kg SO3/ha for each additional 100 kg N/ha

In drought conditions, SO3 uptake will be limited — delay or reduce applications.

## 9. Grass-Clover Swards

For grass-clover swards, N fixation by clover reduces the N fertiliser requirement. See Section 3 of RB209 for specific guidance. In general, where clover makes up >30% of the sward, N fertiliser can be reduced significantly or omitted. Apply P, K and Mg as for pure grass to maintain clover content. Clover is more responsive to K than grass.
